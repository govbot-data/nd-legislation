5 SECTION 3. A new section to chapter 43-15 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
6 and enacted as follows:
7 Prescriptive authority.
8 1. A pharmacist whose practice is physically located within this state, acting in good faith
9 and exercising reasonable care, may independently prescribe drugs, drug categories,
10 and devices as provided in this section if each of the following requirements are met:
11 a. A pharmacist may prescribe drugs or devices only for conditions for which the
12 pharmacist is educationally prepared and competence has been achieved and
13 maintained.
14 b. A pharmacist may issue a prescription only for a legitimate medical purpose
15 arising from a patient-pharmacist relationship.
16 c. A pharmacist shall obtain adequate information about the patient's health status
17 to make appropriate decisions based on the applicable standard of care.
18 d. For each drug or drug category a pharmacist intends to prescribe, the pharmacist
19 shall maintain a patient assessment protocol based on current clinical guidelines,
20 when available, or evidence-based research findings that specify the following:
21 (1) Patient inclusion and exclusion criteria; and
22 (2) Explicit medical referral criteria.
23 e. A pharmacist shall revise the patient assessment protocol when necessary to
24 ensure continued compliance with clinical guidelines or evidence-based research
25 findings. The pharmacist's patient assessment protocol, and any related forms,
26 must be made available to the board upon request.
27 f. A pharmacist shall consult with and refer to other health care professionals as
28 appropriate, including in situations where the pharmacist's knowledge or
29 experience is limited.
30 g. A pharmacist shall develop and implement an appropriate follow-up care plan,
31 including any monitoring parameters, in accordance with clinical guidelines. The

30 SECTION 4. A new section to chapter 43-15 of the North Dakota Century Code is created
31 and enacted as follows:

1 Therapeutic substitution.
2 1. A pharmacist whose practice is physically located within this state may substitute a
3 drug for a therapeutically equivalent drug, except for antidepressants, antipsychotics,
4 chemotherapy agents, schedule II controlled substances, biological products, and
5 narrow therapeutic index drugs, as limited by this section. Therapeutic equivalence
6 may be established by clinical publications comparing dosages of drugs in a
7 therapeutic class.
8 2. A pharmacist may not substitute a drug for a therapeutically equivalent drug if:
9 a. The prescriber indicates no substitution is to be made; or
10 b. The board has determined a therapeutically equivalent drug should not be
11 substituted and notified pharmacists of that determination.
12 3. Before dispensing a therapeutically equivalent drug, a pharmacist shall:
13 a. Verbally discuss the suggested substitution with the patient, including informing
14 the patient that the therapeutically equivalent drug does not contain the identical
15 active ingredient present in the prescribed drug and any differences in dosage
16 and frequency between the prescribed drug and the therapeutically equivalent
17 drug;
18 b. Inform the patient of the patient's right to refuse the substitution; and
19 c. Determine whether the substitution would provide a cost benefit to the patient or
20 provide access if the prescribed drug is not available.
21 4. The pharmacist shall send notice of the substitution to the prescriber by electronic
22 communication within twenty-four hours of dispensing the drug to the patient.
23 5. The prescribing provider is not liable for a substitution made by a pharmacist under
24 this section.
