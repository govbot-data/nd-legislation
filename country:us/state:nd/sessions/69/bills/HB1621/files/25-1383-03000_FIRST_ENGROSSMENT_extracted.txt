1 A BILL for an Act to amend and reenact subsection 1 of section 15.1-21-01 and section
2 15.1-21-02 of the North Dakota Century Code, relating to requiring the presidential physical
3 fitness test in physical education courses in elementary, middle, and high schools and providing
4 exceptions to the test; and to provide an effective date.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY OF NORTH DAKOTA:
6 SECTION 1. AMENDMENT. Subsection 1 of section 15.1-21-01 of the North Dakota
7 Century Code is amended and reenacted as follows:
8 1. Provide to students instruction in:
9 a. English language arts, including reading, composition, creative writing, English
10 grammar, and spelling.
11 b. Mathematics.
12 c. Social studies, including:
13 (1) The United States Constitution;
14 (2) United States history;
15 (3) Geography;
16 (4) Government; and
17 (5) North Dakota studies, with an emphasis on geography, history, the federally
18 recognized Indian tribes in the state, and agriculture of this state, in the
19 fourth and eighth grades.
20 d. Science, including agriculture.
21 e. Physical education, including requiring participation in the presidential physical
22 fitness test. The superintendent of public instruction may establish criteria for and

3 f. Health, including physiology, hygiene, disease control, and the nature and effects

5 g. Computer science, including cybersecurity.
6 SECTION 2. AMENDMENT. Section 15.1-21-02 of the North Dakota Century Code is
7 amended and reenacted as follows:
8 15.1-21-02. High schools - Required units.
9 1. To be approved by the superintendent of public instruction, each public and nonpublic
10 high school shall provide instruction in or make available to each student:
11 a. Four units of English language arts from a sequence that includes literature,
12 composition, and speech;
13 b. Four units of mathematics, including:
14 (1) One unit of algebra II; and
15 (2) One unit for which algebra II is a prerequisite;
16 c. Four units of science, including:
17 (1) One unit of physical science; and
18 (2) One unit of biology;
19 d. Four units of social studies, including:
20 (1) One unit of world history;
21 (2) One unit of United States history, including Native American tribal history;
22 and
23 (3) (a) One unit of problems of democracy; or
24 (b) One-half unit of United States government and one-half unit of
25 economics;
26 e. One-half unit of health;
27 f. One-half unit of physical education during each school year, including requiring
28 participation in the presidential physical fitness test, provided that once every four
29 years the unit must be a concept-based fitness class that includes instruction in
30 the assessment, improvement, and maintenance of personal fitness;
31 g. Two units of fine arts, at least one of which must be music;

1 h. Two units of the same foreign or native American language;
2 i. One unit of an advanced placement course or one unit of a dual-credit course;
3 j. Two units of career and technical education from a coordinated plan of study
4 recommended by the department of career and technical education and
5 approved by the superintendent of public instruction; and
6 k. One unit of computer science or cybersecurity.
7 2. In addition to the requirements of subsection 1, each public and nonpublic high school
8 shall:
9 a. Make available to each student, at least once every two years, one-half unit of
10 North Dakota studies, with an emphasis on the geography, history, and
11 agriculture of this state; and
12 b. Develop a computer science and cybersecurity integration plan to ensure
13 introduction to computer science and cybersecurity knowledge. The board of a
14 public school or school district shall approve a plan by July 1, 2024.
15 3. Each unit which must be made available under this section must meet or exceed the
16 state content standards, unless a school district or governing board of a nonpublic
17 high school has adopted a mastery framework policy and awards units based on the
18 successful completion of the relevant portions of the North Dakota learning continuum.
19 A mastery framework policy adopted by a school district or governing board of a
20 nonpublic high school must identify the portions of the North Dakota learning
21 continuum which must be mastered for a student to attain units necessary for high
22 school graduation under section 15.1-21-02.2.
23 4. For purposes of this section, unless the context otherwise requires, "make available"
24 means that:
25 a. Each public high school and nonpublic high school shall allow students to select
26 units over the course of a high school career from a list that includes at least
27 those required by this section;
28 b. If a student selects a unit from the list required by this section, the public high
29 school or the nonpublic high school shall provide the unit to the student; and
30 c. The unit may be provided to the student through any delivery method not
31 contrary to state law and may include classroom or individual instruction and
