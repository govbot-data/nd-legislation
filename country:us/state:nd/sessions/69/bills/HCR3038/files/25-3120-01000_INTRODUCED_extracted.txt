Introduced by
Representatives Bosch, Christianson, Ista
Senators Axtman, Cleary, Davison, Sickler
1 A concurrent resolution to recognize and congratulate the Young Men's Christian Association on
2 its 175th anniversary.
3 WHEREAS, the Young Men's Christian Association, known as the YMCA and the Y, marks
4 its 175th anniversary in the United States; and
5 WHEREAS, the Young Men's Christian Association was founded in the United States by
6 Thomas Valentine Sullivan in 1851, inspired by the Young Men's Christian Association in
7 England, to put Christian principles into practice by building healthy spirits, minds, and bodies
8 for all; and
9 WHEREAS, as a charitable nonprofit, the Young Men's Christian Association has
10 continually adapted to meet evolving needs of communities, strengthened society by bringing
11 people together, fostered relationships, and created a sense of belonging; and
12 WHEREAS, the legacy of the Young Men's Christian Association includes providing safe
13 housing during the industrial revolution; pioneering the first night school and English as a
14 second language class; inventing basketball; leading humanitarian efforts during wartime,
15 including the work of John Raleigh Mott who received the 1946 Nobel Peace Prize; establishing
16 the youth and government program in 1936 as a premier civic engagement initiative that
17 continues today; becoming the United States' leading nonprofit provider of child care and
18 afterschool programs; and providing food and emergency child care to front-line workers during
19 the SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) pandemic; and
20 WHEREAS, the Young Men's Christian Association serves in every state and territory
21 across the United States, reaching more than 10,000 communities, and is supported by over
22 300,000 staff members and 350,000 volunteers who deliver essential programs to more than
23 17,000,000 individuals annually, including nearly 11,000,000 adults and 6,500,000 children and
24 youth; and

18 to each Young Men's Christian Association in this state.
